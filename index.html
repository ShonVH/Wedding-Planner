The family welcome dinner — Friday 19 December 2026
A structured but relaxed gathering 2 nights before the wedding gives both families time to meet without the pressure of the big day. Both families are within South Africa — this makes logistics manageable and a single-night event sufficient.
Format — Cape Town braai or garden dinner
Host it at a different venue from The Lawns — keep the wedding venue special for the day itself. A private braai or garden dinner at a guesthouse works beautifully. Aim for 6pm arrival, relaxed pace, done by 10pm.
  • Shared platters and a braai encourage mingling far better than a formal menu.
  • Keep it inclusive — children and non-drinkers should feel equally welcome.
  • Background music only — no formal speeches until everyone has settled in.
How to help both families connect
  • Mixed seating deliberately arranged: Do not let SVH sit with SVH and JS with JS. Seat by age group or shared interests.
  • Couple's growing-up slideshow: Run on a TV — childhood photos give everyone something to talk about immediately.
  • Name cards with a conversation starter: "Ask me about..." on each card breaks the ice without pressure.
  • One fun icebreaker: "How well do you know JS & SVH?" — gets everyone laughing across family lines.
  • Brief warm welcomes: Both sets of parents stand and say 2 minutes of welcome — not speeches, just warmth.
  • Welcome gift bag: Local gift (wine, biltong, Cape fudge) for out-of-town guests.
Practical logistics
  • Book a block of rooms at one hotel or guesthouse for both families — shared accommodation builds familiarity overnight.
  • Arrange a shuttle from accommodation to the welcome dinner and back — 30–45 minutes on a bus together is worth more than any icebreaker game.
  • Create a WhatsApp group for the full guest weekend — Friday dinner address, Saturday suggestions, Sunday ceremony details.
  • Appoint one coordinator from each family side to handle queries so you are free to enjoy your weekend.
